Projects

Bayesian nonparametrics models for spatial random graphs

Ongoing work with F. Caron and J. Rousseau on a new model for sparse spatial random graphs, with flexible sparsity levels and power law degree distribution.

Asymptotic properties of graphs based on graphex processes

Ww study the asymptotic properties of random graphs originated from graphex processes, in particular sparsity levels, degree distribution and clustering coefficients. We derive a central limit theorem for the number of nodes and we propose a class of models that can tackle separatly the local and global structures of a network. Joint work with F. Caron and J. Rousseau.

Disclosure risk assessment

We propose a nonparametric estimator to quantify disclosure risk, and we prove its optimality in terms of vanishing normalized mean squared error.
