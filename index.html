About Me


I am a fourth (last!) year DPhil candidate in the Department of Statistics at the University of Oxford, part of the CDT programme OxWaSP between Oxford and Warwick. I am working under the supervision of Prof Fran√ßois Caron and Prof Judith Rousseau. I am a member of St Peter's College and recepient of an EPSRC scholarship.

My research interests lie around Bayesian models for networks, disclosure risk assessment and fair machine learning.

In Spring 2020 I visited Duke University in Durham, NC, collaborating with Prof David Dunson. In Spring 2021 I worked at IDSIA (Dalle Molle Institute for Artificial Intelligence) in Lugano, Switzerland, with Dr Marco Scutari.

Projects

Bayesian nonparametrics models for spatial random graphs

Ongoing work with F. Caron and J. Rousseau on a new model for sparse spatial random graphs, with flexible sparsity levels and power law degree distribution.

Asymptotic properties of graphs based on graphex processes

We study the asymptotic properties of random graphs originated from graphex processes, in particular sparsity levels, degree distribution and clustering coefficients. We derive a central limit theorem for the number of nodes and we propose a class of models that can tackle separatly the local and global structures of a network. Joint work with F. Caron and J. Rousseau.

Disclosure risk assessment

We propose a nonparametric and a Bayesian nonparametric estimators to quantify disclosure risk. We prove the optimality of the former in terms of vanishing normalized mean squared error, and we compute the posterior in close form for the latter.

Fair machine learning

We propose a linear model and a glm to achieve the fair requirement of statistical parity through the use of ridge regression, obtaining a procedure which is fast, easy to use and flexible since it allows for multiple sensitive features (categorical and continuous).
